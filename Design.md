# Turn-Based Tactical Roguelike

Configuration-Driven, Class-Coordinated Design Document (v6)

---

## Vision

A session-based, turn-based tactical roguelike where the player controls a coordinated two-unit squad.

The game is about:

* tactical role coordination
* resource management
* configuration trade-offs

Combat exists to expose weaknesses in role synergy.

---

# Core Design Identity

The project is defined by four pillars:

---

### 1. Role Coordination Over Solo Power

The player controls two distinct classes:

* Tank
* DPS

Victory depends on synergy, not individual strength.

---

### 2. Independent Resource Systems

Each class has its own resource:

* Tank → Guard
* DPS → Momentum

Resources are:

* generated by class-specific actions
* spent for power spikes
* tactically managed

Resources are tools, not the core identity of the game.

---

### 3. Threat Control Layer

Combat includes a visible threat system.

* Damage increases threat
* Taunt is an active ability
* Threat determines enemy targeting
* DPS can over-aggro

The main tactical tension:

> Can you maintain stable threat distribution?

---

### 4. Trade-Off Driven Configuration

Every improvement introduces weakness.

Weapons and armor modify behavior, not just numbers.

If a feature does not reinforce tactical coordination and trade-offs, it does not belong.

---

# Core Gameplay Loop

```
Main Menu
v
Enter Hub
v
Configure Tank + DPS
v
Generate Dungeon
v
Room -> Resolve Encounter
v
Evaluate Outcome
v
Return to Hub
v
Adjust Configuration
```

The loop must remain stable.

# Classes

## Tank

Role: Stabilizer & Threat Controller

Core mechanics:

* Block generates Guard
* Taunt increases threat toward Tank
* Guard improves mitigation or strengthens taunt
* Low damage scaling

Failure condition:
If Tank mismanages Guard or taunt timing, DPS dies.

---

## DPS

Role: Pressure & Burst

Core mechanics:

* Attacks generate Momentum
* Momentum increases crit chance or burst damage
* High damage scaling
* Low survivability

Failure condition:
If DPS over-extends, they pull aggro and collapse.

---

# Resource Systems

## Guard (Tank)

Generated by:

* Block
* Defensive abilities
* Certain armor modifiers

Spent on:

* Enhanced Taunt
* Damage reduction
* Reactive mitigation

---

## Momentum (DPS)

Generated by:

* Attacks
* Crits
* Certain weapon modifiers

Spent on:

* Burst strike
* Critical amplification
* Finisher abilities

---

# Threat System

Each enemy tracks threat values per player unit.

Threat increases by:

* Damage dealt
* Healing (optional tuning)
* Taunt (large flat increase)

Enemy targets the highest threat unit.

Threat can decay slightly each round (tunable).

This creates tension:

* DPS must manage burst timing
* Tank must reassert control

---

# Skill Structure & Role Expression

Each class expresses identity through a constrained ability structure.

Maximum per class:

* 1 Passive
* 2 Active abilities
* 1 Ultimate

Skills are granted through equipment configuration.

There is no shared ability pool.
There is no cross-class skill mixing.
There is no vertical scaling of skill count.

Abilities reinforce identity and trade-offs.

---

## Role Expression Model

### Tank

Role identity: Stabilization & Threat Control

Abilities must:

* interact with Guard
* interact with Threat
* reinforce defensive timing
* stabilize volatile situations

Tank abilities do not create burst dominance.
Tank controls tempo, not damage spikes.

---

### DPS

Role identity: Pressure & Volatility

Abilities must:

* interact with Momentum
* create burst windows
* increase risk-reward tension
* amplify timing importance

DPS abilities do not stabilize combat.
They increase pressure.

---

# Ability Categories

## Passive

* Always active
* Modifies resource behavior, mitigation, or threat dynamics
* Shapes identity but does not create new actions

Passives define playstyle bias.

---

## Active

* Player-triggered
* Costs resource (Guard or Momentum)
* Creates tactical decision point
* Must have opportunity cost

Actives are timing tools.

---

## Ultimate

* High resource cost
* High tactical impact
* Limited frequency
* Designed to resolve unstable states

Ultimate amplifies identity but does not replace it.

---

# Equipment Philosophy

Equipment changes behavior, not just numbers.

Equipment grants:

* Passive
* Active slots
* Ultimate

Equipment defines a behavior package.

Stronger equipment does not increase stat inflation.
It increases behavioral complexity.

Trade-offs remain mandatory.

No rarity tiers.
No loot inflation.

---

# Combat Philosophy

Combat is:

* Turn-based
* Initiative-driven
* Deterministic core with controlled randomness
* Fully logged in text

Combat tests:

* Role synergy
* Resource timing
* Threat stability
* Configuration coherence

---

# Level-Up & Trade-Offs

Choices are mutually exclusive.

Tank examples:

* +Guard generation, -damage
* Stronger Taunt, slower initiative
* Higher mitigation, lower max HP

DPS examples:

* Higher crit scaling, lower base damage
* Faster initiative, weaker sustain
* More Momentum gain, lower defense

No pure stat growth.

Every choice narrows identity.

---

# Dungeon Structure (MVP)

* 5–10 rooms
* First room safe
* Final room boss
* No spatial map required

Room types:

* Combat
* Event
* Treasure
* Boss

---

# Enemy Philosophy

Enemies stress different axes:

* Burst enemies punish weak tanking
* Defensive enemies punish low DPS output
* Fast enemies punish poor initiative builds
* Aggressive enemies punish threat mismanagement

Enemy AI remains readable:

* Aggressive
* Defensive
* Desperate

Health-threshold driven.

# Intended Player Reflection

After a failed run, the player should think:

> I mismanaged threat.

or

> I mistimed my burst.

or

> My Tank and DPS builds lacked synergy.

If they think:

> The numbers were random.

The design has failed.
